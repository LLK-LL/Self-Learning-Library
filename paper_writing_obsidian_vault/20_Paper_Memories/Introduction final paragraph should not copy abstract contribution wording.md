# Introduction final paragraph should not copy abstract contribution wording

The introduction's final paragraph can expand the abstract's research logic, but it should not repeat the abstract's contribution wording.

When closing the introduction, use a research-transition or study-scope expression rather than copying abstract-style claims such as "improve mechanical performance", "guide process parameter design", or "provide a numerical prediction tool".

If two mechanisms do not have a direct causal relation, do not compress them into one chain. Summarize them as parallel or sequential research tasks.
