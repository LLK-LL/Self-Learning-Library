# Theory Formula Descriptions Require Textual Connectors And Formatted Indices

For theory-chapter formula descriptions, prose should avoid operator-like text notation. Method and material combinations in explanatory sentences should be written with textual connectors, for example `SPH and FEM` rather than `SPH-FEM`, and `TC4 and Al6061` rather than `TC4/Al6061`, when the text is descriptive rather than a formal formula or reference title.

Variables in explanatory prose should not be typed with raw underscores or caret braces. They should use Word's real subscript or superscript formatting. Variable bodies should be italic. Descriptive labels or abbreviations used as indices, such as `V`, `max`, `eff`, and `SF`, should remain upright, while variable-like single-letter indices may remain italic when they function as mathematical indices.

Boundary: this rule applies to theory-chapter formula descriptions and explanatory prose. It does not require rewriting formula bodies, DOI strings, reference titles, journal metadata, or already-approved non-theory prose unless the user explicitly expands the scope.
