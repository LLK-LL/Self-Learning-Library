# Mentor-Level Literature Summaries Require Accurate Elegant Varied Expression

## User Supplement

For the mentor-version introduction logic, add a higher-weight requirement: when summarizing references in the introduction, each cited paper must be understood from its abstract and actual contribution before being rewritten into the manuscript. The summary should not merely copy keywords or mechanically state that an author "used characterization and testing".

## Rule Meaning

Reference summaries should satisfy `信、达、雅`:

- `信`: accurately reflect the cited paper's research object, method, and conclusion or contribution.
- `达`: express the paper's role in the current paragraph clearly, so readers understand why the citation appears there.
- `雅`: use concise, formal, and fluent academic Chinese rather than awkward translation, vague phrasing, or repeated templates.

In one paragraph, adjacent citation sentences should not repeatedly use the same pattern, such as repeatedly writing `采用...研究了...分析了...`. If several references use similar methods, the paragraph should vary sentence structure, merge related studies where appropriate, or shift emphasis among object, method, finding, and limitation.

## Reusable Requirement

For future introduction revisions, Codex should first understand each reference's abstract-level content and then write a varied literature-review sentence. The paragraph should progress through evidence and logic, not through repetitive citation templates.
