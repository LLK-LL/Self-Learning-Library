# User correction - reduce overloaded adjective and noun modifier chains in SCI English

## Memory

The user corrected two translated abstract sentences because they contained too many adjectives and noun modifiers, which increased reading complexity:

> The simulated range of the high-temperature molten zone is basically consistent with the experimentally measured thickness of the intermetallic compound layer, with a relative error of 8%. The error between the interface-wave dimensions measured by metallographic microscopy and those obtained by simulation is 5%.

The correction requires future SCI English translation to reduce dense modifier chains and express comparisons more directly. The scientific meaning must be preserved: the simulated high-temperature molten zone should be compared with the experimentally measured intermetallic compound layer, and the interface-wave size from simulation should be compared with the metallographic measurement. The numerical errors `8%` and `5%` must remain unchanged.
